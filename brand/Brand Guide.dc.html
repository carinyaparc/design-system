The Carinya Parc identity

How we look, sound and feel — a peaceful home for land, food and community, on 42 hectares at The Branch, NSW. Use this guide to keep everything we make warm, grounded and unmistakably ours.

01

The story

The name

Carinya means
“peaceful home.”

An Aboriginal word carried into the paddocks of The Branch. Everything we make should feel like arriving somewhere restful, generous and alive — a home the land is healing into.

Positioning

A working regenerative farm you’re invited into — where healing degraded land, growing nutrient-dense food and welcoming people are one act of care.

Mission

To heal land and food systems through hands-on ecological restoration, nutrient-dense produce, and collective action.

Personality
Warm & welcoming Grounded & authentic Rugged & hands-on Quietly playful
Values
Regeneration over extraction. Leave it healthier than we found it.
Stewardship over ownership. The land is borrowed from those to come.
Collaboration & transparency. Many hands, open sharing.
Voice & tone — warm, first-person, in Jonno’s words

“We treat the land as borrowed from our grandkids.”

“Come get your hands dirty — every tree counts.”

Do — speak plainly, first person, invite people in.
Don’t — greenwash, lecture, or hide behind jargon.

05

Graphic language

Soft shapes

Over-rounded corners (20–28px), pills, circles and organic blobs. No sharp corners or hairlines.

The arc device

A rising-sun / hill arc frames headlines and section tops — horizon, growth, a new day.

06

Photography

Real land, real hands, real light. Golden-hour warmth, wide horizons and honest close-ups — never sterile stock. Round the corners; let the warm paper frame it.

Warm & golden — shoot early or late; embrace the dry-grass gold.
People & hands — show the doing: planting, feeding, harvesting.
Rounded corners — always ≥18px; never hard rectangles.
