01 · The name
Carinya means
“peaceful home.”

An Aboriginal word carried into the paddocks of the Upper Hunter. Everything we make should feel like arriving somewhere restful, generous and alive.

A home the land is healing into — 42 hectares of woodland, habitat corridors and productive agroforestry in the making.

01 · The story
Positioning

A working regenerative farm you’re invited into — where healing land, growing food and welcoming people are one act of care.

Mission

To heal land and food systems through hands-on ecological restoration, nutrient-dense produce and collective action.

Personality & values
Warm & welcoming Grounded & authentic Rugged & hands-on Quietly playful
Regeneration
over extraction

Leave it healthier than we found it.

Stewardship
over ownership

The land is borrowed from those to come.

Collaboration
& transparency

Many hands, open sharing.

04 · Voice & tone — warm, first-person, in Jonno’s words

“We treat the land as borrowed from our grandkids.”

“Come get your hands dirty — every tree counts.”

Do — speak plainly, first person, invite people in.
Don’t — greenwash, lecture, or hide behind jargon.
